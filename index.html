I'm Sarita Baker — a learning and development professional with over 10 years of experience designing engaging, learner-centered programs for children and adults.

I came to Spanish enrichment through a deep belief that language learning should be joyful, not stressful. My background in instructional design, curriculum development, and child-centered facilitation shapes every class I teach.

I bring a warm, play-based approach to instruction with a strong emphasis on confidence-building, curiosity, and relationship-centered learning. As a parent, I'm especially attuned to the developmental needs of young learners.

My teaching philosophy

Joy before correctness

A child who is laughing and trying will outlearn a child who is silent and afraid of being wrong. I create environments where mistakes are part of the fun.

The body is the classroom

Vocabulary that moves through the body — through dance, gesture, game — sticks far longer than vocabulary that sits in a workbook.

Every learner has a door

Some kids talk first. Some draw first. Some need to watch before they try. I design for all of them — so every child has a way in.

Language is culture

Spanish isn't just vocabulary lists. It's Las Escondidas at the park, ordering at a taco stand, greeting an abuela. Context makes it real.
